Getting paid quickly is one of the most important parts of running a freelance business. The right invoicing tool makes the process fast, professional, and painless. {{< vendor-link "freshbooks" />}} and {{< vendor-link "wave" />}} are two of the most popular options for freelancers in 2026, but they serve different needs.

## FreshBooks: Best for Growing Freelancers

FreshBooks is a full-featured accounting and invoicing platform built specifically for self-employed professionals and small businesses. It handles invoicing, expense tracking, time tracking, and basic accounting in one place.

**Pricing:** Lite plan at $17/month for up to 5 clients. Plus at $30/month for up to 50 clients. Premium at $55/month for unlimited clients.

**Best for:** Freelancers who want a professional all-in-one solution and are willing to pay for quality.

**Pros:** Beautiful invoice templates, automatic payment reminders, expense tracking with receipt capture, time tracking built in, excellent customer support.

**Cons:** No free plan, pricing scales with number of clients.

## Wave: Best Free Option

Wave is a completely free accounting and invoicing platform. It earns revenue through payment processing fees rather than subscription charges. For freelancers who send a moderate number of invoices, it is hard to beat free.

**Pricing:** Free for invoicing and accounting. Payment processing fees apply when clients pay online (2.9% plus $0.60 per transaction).

**Best for:** Freelancers starting out or those who want professional invoicing without a monthly subscription.

**Pros:** Completely free, unlimited invoices, professional templates, integrated payments, basic accounting included.

**Cons:** Limited customer support, fewer integrations than FreshBooks, payment processing fees add up on large invoices.
